Recommended primary meta-features (balanced efficiency + rigor)
total_measurement_events
Curation: For each feature family, take the maximum of its count variants across cadences (e.g., _mean_count, _mean_count_6h), then sum across families. Avoids double-counting the same underlying events at different windows.
Meaning: Overall measurement density per stay (how much was observed).
Notes: Optionally log1p-transform for robustness to heavy tails.
unique_feature_count
Curation: Count of distinct feature families with any nonzero count (post cadence-deduplication). Anchor family list to the Phase II blueprint to stabilize denominators across runs.
Meaning: Breadth of measured variable types (coverage of domains).
Notes: Validate mapping with an allowlist from Phase II to prevent naming drift.
imputation_proportion
Curation: For the canonical family set, proportion with max count == 0 (never observed in window), i.e., fully imputed families; denominator is the fixed canonical family count.
Meaning: Share of the representation inferred rather than observed.
Notes: Drop total_imputation_count (redundant with proportion; reduces multiple testing).
aggregate_stddev
Curation: Per-family average of its stddev* columns (to avoid overweighting families with more derived columns), then mean across families present. No cross-patient standardization required.
Meaning: Average within-stay volatility across signals.
Notes: Report vitals-only and labs-only sensitivity analyses as secondary if needed, but keep a single combined primary metric.
aggregate_slope (trend magnitude)
Curation: Prefer 24h slope where available else 6h; take absolute value per column; per-family average; then mean across families present.
Meaning: Average magnitude of within-stay trend (how strongly variables are changing).
Notes: Using magnitude avoids sign cancellation and zero-imputation dilution.
input_character_count
Curation: Use file byte size as primary fast proxy; fallback to decoded text length on read failure; store log1p-scaled values. Report percent with text coverage.
Meaning: Proxy for documentation volume (textual data density).
Notes: Keep as exploratory in the density family; retain current efficient approach.
Features to remove (net benefit limited or redundant)
total_imputation_count
Rationale: Highly collinear with imputation_proportion; proportion is scale-free and easier to compare across cohorts.
Family grouping for per-family FDR (unchanged)
density: total_measurement_events, unique_feature_count, input_character_count
volatility: aggregate_stddev, aggregate_slope
imputation: imputation_proportion
Short confidence notes per feature
total_measurement_events: High (with cadence-dedup via per-family max).
unique_feature_count: High (with canonical family set).
imputation_proportion: High (with fixed denominator from blueprint).
aggregate_stddev: Medium-High (family-wise averaging mitigates weighting artifacts).
aggregate_slope (magnitude): Medium-High (magnitude and cadence preference improve robustness).
input_character_count (log): Medium (proxy is noisy but informative; log scaling and coverage reporting mitigate risks).
